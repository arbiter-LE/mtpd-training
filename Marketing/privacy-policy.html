Privacy Policy

Effective June 6, 2026 · arbiterle.com

This policy describes how Arbiter LE LLC collects, uses, and protects information in connection with the Arbiter LE law enforcement training platform. It applies to all users, including agency administrators and enrolled officers.

1. Information We Collect

Officer Account Information

When an agency enrolls officers on the Platform, Arbiter LE collects the following for each officer:

  • Name and rank
  • Badge number (used as the account identifier)
  • Role designation (officer or administrator)
  • Department affiliation

Training Activity Data

The Platform automatically records the following as officers complete training:

  • Module completion status and completion dates
  • Quiz scores (last score, best score, and score history)
  • Number of training attempts per module
  • Time spent on each module
  • Scenario decision paths and outcomes
  • Remediation status

Technical and Usage Data

Arbiter LE may collect standard technical data including device type, browser type, IP address, and session duration for platform security and stability monitoring. This data is not linked to individual officer records in reports.

2. How We Use Information

Arbiter LE uses collected information solely for the following purposes:

  • Authenticating officer and administrator logins
  • Recording and displaying officer training progress within the Platform
  • Generating compliance reports accessible to authorized agency administrators
  • Improving platform performance, reliability, and training content quality
  • Communicating with agency administrators regarding platform status, updates, or technical issues

Arbiter LE does not use officer data for advertising, marketing to individual officers, or sale to third parties under any circumstances.

3. Aggregated and De-Identified Data

Arbiter LE may use aggregated, de-identified data — data that cannot reasonably identify any individual officer or agency — for internal research, product improvement, and industry benchmarking. This data will never include names, badge numbers, or any other individually identifying information.

4. Data Storage and Security

Officer data is stored in a cloud-hosted database with row-level security controls that prevent any officer from accessing another officer's records. Data is encrypted in transit using TLS and encrypted at rest.

Access to officer data is limited to: (1) the officer whose data it is, (2) authorized agency administrators within that officer's department, and (3) Arbiter LE personnel with a legitimate operational need.

Arbiter LE implements reasonable and industry-standard technical and organizational security measures to protect against unauthorized access, disclosure, or destruction of officer data.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

Arbiter LE does not sell, rent, or trade officer data. We may disclose data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • To the agency that enrolled the officer, through the administrative dashboard
  • To third-party service providers under confidentiality agreements, strictly as necessary to operate the Platform
  • If required by law, court order, or valid legal process
  • To protect the rights, safety, or property of Arbiter LE or the public in an emergency

6. Data Retention

Arbiter LE retains officer training records for the duration of the agency's active service agreement, plus thirty (30) days following termination to allow for data export. Upon written request, Arbiter LE will delete all officer records associated with that agency within fifteen (15) business days.

7. Officer Rights

Officers whose data is processed on the Platform have the following rights, exercisable through their agency administrator:

  • Right to access: obtain a copy of training records associated with their badge number
  • Right to correction: request correction of inaccurate records
  • Right to deletion: request deletion of records upon departure from the department or program termination

Requests should be directed to the agency administrator, who may contact Arbiter LE at the address in Section 9.

8. Changes to This Policy

Arbiter LE may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated to agency administrators at least fourteen (14) days prior to taking effect. Continued use of the Platform after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.
