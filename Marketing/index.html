Why It Matters

Inconsistency Is Liability

Inconsistent training isn't just an operational problem — it's a legal one.

When an officer is involved in a use-of-force incident, a pursuit, or a domestic violence response, one of the first questions asked is: what was this officer trained to do, and can you prove it? If the answer is "we trained everyone — we just can't document it," that's exposure.

Small departments face this disproportionately. Without the budget for regular scenario-based training, officers learn inconsistently — from each other, from experience, from whatever resources happen to be available. The standard varies officer to officer. So does the risk.

Arbiter LE gives every officer in your department access to the same high-quality, scenario-driven training — and gives command staff the records to prove it happened.

About

Built From the Inside

Arbiter LE LLC was built by an active municipal law enforcement officer with over 14 years of experience — including time as a Field Training Officer responsible for preparing the next generation of officers to do the job right.

The problem was simple and frustrating: small departments want to train their people to a high standard, but scenario-based training is expensive. Outside instructors, facility time, scheduling across shifts — it adds up fast, and most small departments don't have the budget. So training becomes inconsistent. Some officers get it, some don't. Nobody can prove who was trained on what, or when.

Arbiter LE exists to close that gap — professional, scenario-driven training that small departments can actually afford, with the documentation to back it up.

Common Questions

What Departments Ask

The questions chiefs and command staff raise most often before bringing Arbiter LE to their department.

How accurate is the policy and legal content?

Your department's general orders and SOPs appear verbatim — never reworded, simplified, or paraphrased. Scenarios use your jurisdiction's real roads and conditions. Any court case referenced is verified against the actual ruling before it ever reaches an officer. The content is built to the standard the work demands.

What happens to my officers' data, and who can see it?

Your department runs on its own private, isolated database — your data never mixes with another agency's. Each officer sees only their own record; command staff see only their own department. Training records are never sold, shared, or used to train any outside system or AI. Your policies and your data stay yours.

Will my policies or data be shared with other agencies?

No. Every department is fully walled off. Your SOPs are used only to build your department's training, and nothing crosses between agencies. Each engagement stands entirely on its own.

How do I know officers actually completed the training?

Every module pairs decision-based scenarios with a knowledge check drawn directly from that module's material. The command dashboard tracks completions, scores, and attempts automatically — giving you a clear, defensible record of who was trained on what, and when.

Does this replace our own policies or training?

No — it reinforces them. Arbiter LE helps officers engage with and retain the policies you already have in place. It never replaces your department's orders or an officer's judgment, and it never invents policy.

Who builds the platform?

Arbiter LE is built from inside the profession — by an active law enforcement officer who has worked the road under the same kind of policies your officers do. That perspective is built into every scenario, so the training reflects the realities of the job rather than a generic, off-the-shelf course.

How does pricing work?

Straightforward and transparent: a flat rate per department — no per-seat charges and no surprise add-ons — with a short initial term so you can see it work before any longer commitment. Reach out and we'll put a clear quote in front of you.
