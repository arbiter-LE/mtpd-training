About

Built From the Inside

Arbiter LE LLC was built by an active municipal law enforcement officer with over 14 years of experience — including time as a Field Training Officer responsible for preparing the next generation of officers to do the job right.

The problem was simple and frustrating: small departments want to train their people to a high standard, but scenario-based training is expensive. Outside instructors, facility time, scheduling across shifts — it adds up fast, and most small departments don't have the budget. So training becomes inconsistent. Some officers get it, some don't. Nobody can prove who was trained on what, or when.

Arbiter LE exists to close that gap — professional, scenario-driven training that small departments can actually afford, with the documentation to back it up.

Why It Matters

Inconsistency Is Liability

Inconsistent training isn't just an operational problem — it's a legal one.

When an officer is involved in a use-of-force incident, a pursuit, or a domestic violence response, one of the first questions asked is: what was this officer trained to do, and can you prove it? If the answer is "we trained everyone — we just can't document it," that's exposure.

Small departments face this disproportionately. Without the budget for regular scenario-based training, officers learn inconsistently — from each other, from experience, from whatever resources happen to be available. The standard varies officer to officer. So does the risk.

Arbiter LE gives every officer in your department access to the same high-quality, scenario-driven training — and gives command staff the records to prove it happened.

The Platform

Scenario-Based. SOP-Driven. Always On.

Arbiter LE is an online scenario-based training platform built specifically for law enforcement.

Officers work through real-world scenarios — use of force, search and seizure, domestic violence response, pursuits, crisis intervention, and more — and your department's SOPs are woven directly into each one. Instead of officers reading policy in a vacuum, they encounter it in context, applied to situations they actually face on the job.

The goal is less than 15 minutes a week, designed to fit into a shift without pulling anyone off the road.
