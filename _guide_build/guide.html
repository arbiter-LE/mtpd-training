Welcome to Your Training Platform

This guide walks through every part of the Arbiter LE platform — screen by screen, button by button. Part 1 covers the officer experience. Part 2 covers the tools available to command staff and supervisors.

Part 1

For Officers

1 · Signing In

Open a web browser and go to egpd.arbiterle.com. You'll see your department's secure sign-in screen.

  1. Enter your Department Email — the EGPD address on file for you.
  2. Enter your Password (set when you first activated your account — see Section 2 if you haven't yet).
  3. Press SIGN IN.
The platform remembers your session on your own device, so you usually won't have to type your password every time. On a shared computer, always use SIGN OUT when you're done so the next person doesn't land in your account.
The sign-in screen. Department email, password, and the Sign In button. The Forgot password? link is just below.

2 · Resetting Your Password

Use this the first time you log in, or any time you forget your password.

  1. On the sign-in screen, type your department email into the Department Email field.
  2. Click Forgot password?. You'll see the message "Reset link sent — check your email."
  3. Open the email from Arbiter LE and click the reset link. It opens the Set New Password screen.
  4. Enter a new password twice (at least 8 characters), then press UPDATE PASSWORD. You'll go straight to your dashboard.
Requesting a reset. Enter your email, click Forgot password?, and the confirmation appears beneath the Sign In button.
Setting a new password. Enter it twice and press Update Password. Back to sign in returns you to the login screen.

3 · Your Training Dashboard

This is your home screen. Everything you need to do is here.

The four summary cards

  • Assigned — total modules assigned to you.
  • Completed — modules you've passed.
  • Overdue — anything past its due date that still needs attention.
  • Avg. Score — your average across passed modules.

The two tabs

TRAINING MODULES shows every module as a card. MY PROGRESS shows your completed record in a table (Section 4).

Reading a module card

Each card shows the topic category, title, and a short description. The colored badge and button tell you its status:

CompletePassed. The button reads ✓ Review Module — you can revisit it any time.
AvailableStart Module → — open and begin.
Due / BufferOpen now, with a due date approaching. Don't let these slip to overdue.
Opens [date]Locked until its scheduled open date — it unlocks automatically.
RemediationThree attempts used without passing. Your supervisor must review and authorize a retake.
The dashboard. Summary cards across the top, progress bar, the two tabs, and module cards in each status.

4 · Tracking Your Progress

Click the MY PROGRESS tab for a clean record of everything you've completed: each module's best score, attempts used, time spent, and the date you finished.

My Progress. Your completed modules with best score, attempts, time, and completion date.

5 · Working Through a Module — Reading

Tap Start Module on any available card. Every module opens with its reading content — the law, your department's policy, and the principles the scenario will test.

  • Case-law and policy boxes highlight the controlling authority. Read these carefully — the quiz draws only from this material.
  • The Reading Time clock in the corner tracks your time; it's informational.
  • In the top bar: 📖 REFERENCE opens the reference library, FLAG ISSUE reports a content problem (Section 12), and ← Dashboard returns home.
  • Scroll to the bottom and press Proceed to Scenario → when you're ready.
Module reading. Scenario framing, core principles, and verified case-law boxes. Controls sit in the top bar.

6 · The Scenario Exercise

The scenario drops you into a realistic call. It opens with a scene card — time, conditions, your assignment, and location — followed by the situation as it unfolds. Press Proceed to Situation → to continue.

The opening scene. Context for the call before any decisions are required.

7 · Making Decisions (and Pausing)

At each decision point, you'll read the situation and choose from the numbered options. Pick the response you'd make on the street — the platform shows you the consequence of that choice and the legal reasoning behind it before moving on.

  • Click the option you'd choose. The scenario responds, then continues to the next decision.
  • ⏸ PAUSE (top right) saves your place and returns you to the dashboard. When you come back, a banner offers to pick up right where you left off — handy if a call comes in mid-training.
  • 📖 REFERENCE opens the reference library without losing your place.
A decision point. The situation, the numbered options, and the Pause / Reference controls in the top bar.

8 · The Debrief

After the final decision, the debrief reviews the path you took. Each decision is marked — Sound Decision, Error Identified, or noted — with the key legal principles summarized. From here you can ← Review Module or Proceed to Knowledge Assessment →.

The debrief. Your decision record with outcome badges, plus a legal-principles recap before the quiz.

9 · The Knowledge Check

The assessment confirms what you took from the module. Every question is answerable from the reading you just completed.

  • Read the scenario and question, then select an answer (A–D).
  • The platform tells you immediately whether you were right or wrong and explains why, revealing the correct answer.
  • Press Next Question → to continue; on the last question it reads Submit Assessment →.
  • ⏸ PAUSE saves your progress and returns you to the dashboard — your place is held for when you come back.
  • The running Score and progress bar track how you're doing. You need 70% to pass.
A quiz question. Question counter, running score, answer choices, and the Pause / Reference controls.
Immediate feedback. Your answer is marked, the correct choice is highlighted, and the reasoning is explained.

10 · Your Results

When you submit, you'll see your score, whether you passed, and a breakdown of correct answers and time spent.

Passed (70%+)Your score is recorded and the module shows Complete on your dashboard.
Did not passYou can retake it — the dots show how many of your three attempts you've used.
Three attempts usedThe module flags for remediation. Your supervisor reviews your record and authorizes a retake before you continue.
Results. Score ring, attempt indicators, and your correct/total/time summary. Return to Dashboard takes you home.

11 · The Reference Library

The 📖 REFERENCE button — available throughout the platform — opens a searchable library of the cases, statutes, terms, and policy references used in training. Use the search box or filter by Case Law, Terms, or SOPs.

Reference & glossary. Search and filter verified legal citations and key terms any time.

12 · Flagging a Content Issue

If you spot something that looks wrong — a citation, a date, a policy reference — use FLAG ISSUE in the top bar. Choose a category, describe the problem (include the module name and what specifically needs correction), and press Submit. Flags go straight to the platform administrators for review.

Flag a content issue. Category plus a short description is all it takes. This keeps the training accurate for everyone.

13 · Platform Use & Disclaimer

The Platform Use & Disclaimer link at the bottom of every screen explains what the platform is — and isn't. In short: it's a training and professional-development tool, not legal advice and not a substitute for your department's policies or your chain of command. On a real incident, your supervisor, your department's official policy, and the District Attorney's Office have final say.

Platform Use & Disclaimer. Always one click away in the footer.
Part 2

For Administrators & Supervisors

Command staff accounts open to the Administration dashboard instead of the officer view. From here you can monitor compliance across the department, review records, and generate reports.

14 · The Administration Dashboard

The top of the dashboard summarizes the whole department at a glance:

  • Total Officers, Fully Compliant (passed everything assigned), Overdue, Completion Rate, and Avg Score.
  • Four tabs organize the detail: Compliance Overview, Module Summary, Legal Citations, and Officer Feedback.
  • BROWSE MODULES (top right) lets you preview the training as officers see it (Section 18).
Administration dashboard. Department-wide stats, the four tabs, the status filter, and the roster table.

15 · Reading the Roster

The Compliance Overview table lists every officer with badge number, modules assigned and completed (with attempt notes), average score, last activity, and a status badge — Complete, In Progress, Overdue, or Remediation. Officers in remediation are highlighted so they're easy to spot. Use the Filter dropdown to narrow the list by status.

16 · Module Summary

The Module Summary tab flips the view from officers to modules: for each module you see how many officers are assigned, the completion rate, the average score, and its due-date status. This is the fastest way to see which topics the department is keeping up with — and which need a nudge.

Module Summary. Completion rate and average score per module, with due-date status at a glance.

17 · Printing a Compliance Report

Press PRINT COMPLIANCE REPORT on the Compliance Overview tab. The platform opens a clean, date-stamped report in a new tab — the full roster, per-module scores in a grid, and department summary statistics. Use your browser's print dialog to print it or save it as a PDF for your records, accreditation files, or a chief's briefing.

Tip. Generated on demand, the report always reflects the current state of training — a reliable snapshot for any given date.

18 · Previewing Modules

BROWSE MODULES switches you into the officer view so you can read any module exactly as your officers will — including modules that haven't opened yet, which carry an Admin Preview banner. A ← Back to Admin button returns you to the dashboard. This is how you review content before a module goes live.

19 · The Supervisor Track

Supervisors receive an expanded version of each module: an additional command-lens reading section and a separate set of graded supervisor questions, on top of the standard patrol content. Admin accounts can preview either version using the Viewing as toggle (Patrol / Supervisor) that appears on the dashboard and module screens — useful for reviewing both tracks before assigning them.

20 · Legal Citations & Officer Feedback

The Legal Citations tab lists every case and statute used across the platform with its verification status, so command staff can confirm the legal basis of any module at a glance. The Officer Feedback tab collects the content issues officers flag (Section 12), so corrections can be reviewed and actioned.
